================================================================
LETHE — HACKATHON DEMO SPEECH
3-minute version (+notes for 2-min compress / 4-min stretch)
================================================================

TL;DR (memorize this one line):
  "Lethe audits medical bills with three AI agents that talk to each
   other peer-to-peer, reach consensus, prove what they did on three
   chains, and email the appeal — without ever keeping your bill."


----------------------------------------------------------------
[ 00:00 — 00:25 ]  HOOK
(stand still, slow, look up at the room)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Eighty percent of medical bills contain errors — almost always
against the patient. Duplicate charges, wrong modifiers, services
that never happened.

When patients dispute, three out of four win. The catch? Most
people never dispute. Forty percent say they don't know how.

And the few apps that try to help make a different problem worse:
they upload your records to a central database and keep them
forever. That's exactly what a HIPAA-anxious patient is trying to
avoid.


----------------------------------------------------------------
[ 00:25 — 00:40 ]  POSITIONING
(a beat faster, more confident)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Lethe runs on a different deal: ephemeral PHI, persistent learning.

You drop a bill in. Three independent AI agents analyze it. They
talk peer-to-peer over a real Gensyn AXL mesh, reach consensus,
draft a formal appeal letter, and email it to your provider.

Your bill never touches storage. Never reaches a model provider.
After about a minute, it's gone.


----------------------------------------------------------------
[ 00:40 — 02:30 ]  LIVE DEMO
(switch to dashboard. Talk while clicking.)
----------------------------------------------------------------

>>> CLICK: a sample bill chip on /dashboard

This is a real ER bill — synthetic, no patient data. Notice the
ten stages running live across the top. Parse and redact happen
locally. Now three terminals open — α GPT-4o, β Claude, γ Gemini.

>>> WHILE TOKENS STREAM:

Each agent is reading the same redacted payload independently.
They don't share a scratchpad. They have different blind spots —
that's the point.

>>> WHEN "exchange" lights up:

Watch this. Each agent broadcasts its findings to the other two
over Gensyn AXL — real ed25519 peer IDs, real POST /send, real
GET /recv. Open the /axl page on the second monitor and you can
see every packet crossing between sidecars.

>>> WHEN "reflect" lights up:

Now each agent runs a SECOND LLM call with peer findings as new
context. They can add findings they missed. They can downgrade
ones their peers convinced them were wrong. The final consensus
runs on the round-two votes — every conclusion survived peer
review.

>>> WHEN "anchor" lights up:

This is where the chains light up. SHA-256 of the bill plus the
verdict gets anchored on 0G Galileo. The full anonymized record
goes into 0G Storage with a merkle root. KeeperHub mirrors the
same anchor to Sepolia. If the verdict was "dispute," KeeperHub
fires a SECOND workflow that records the dispute on its own
Sepolia contract. Three chains, three independent records, one
audit. Receipts include every transaction hash — verifiable on
the matching block explorer.

>>> WHEN the receipt appears:

Here's the receipt. Five chain transaction hashes. The drafted
appeal letter is right here.

>>> CLICK "Edit draft":

Patient fills in their name, address, the provider's email — these
are structured inputs that auto-format into a proper letter. Live
preview below shows exactly what gets sent.

>>> CLICK "Send appeal":

Email goes out via Resend right now — to my own inbox. KeeperHub
fires a THIRD workflow recording on-chain that the appeal was sent.
The recipient address is keccak-hashed before going on-chain — the
provider's email never appears as plaintext on Sepolia.

>>> SHOW the email arriving in inbox.

That's the audit. Everything you just saw, anchored on three
chains, drafted by three different LLMs, delivered to the provider,
and the patient's bill never persisted anywhere we control.


----------------------------------------------------------------
[ 02:30 — 02:55 ]  WHY IT MATTERS — sponsor track flex
(slower, look up)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Three pillars, three sponsors, all real:

ZERO. The architecture is the privacy guarantee. Bill bytes zeroed
from memory after parsing. SSE events carry stage names only — no
content. We can't leak what we don't keep.

ONE. Gensyn AXL: three Docker sidecars with cryptographic peer
identities, live mesh, no central broker. The round-two reflection
literally cannot fire without findings crossing the mesh.

TWO. 0G stack: Chain anchors the verdicts, Storage holds the full
records, Compute can run one of the agents on decentralized
inference. Three pillars wired, not one.

THREE. KeeperHub: three distinct workflow executions per audit —
mirror anchor, dispute filing, appeal-sent attestation. Different
contracts, different gates, all retried and gas-optimized
automatically.


----------------------------------------------------------------
[ 02:55 — 03:05 ]  CLOSE
(slow, look up, hold the wordmark)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Lethe — medical bills, audited by AI consensus.

Forgotten by design.

[smile, step back]

  + (~25s) The bidirectional storage beat. "0G Storage isn't cold

    feeds the full structured records — voter agent names, full
    code strings — into the next reasoning pass. Storage is the
    primary memory layer; the chain index is just a queryable
    pointer."
